Microfluidics

Microfluidics refers to the behavior, precise control, and manipulation of fluids
geometrically constrained to a micro- or nano-scales for which capillary governs mass transport. 
Microfluidics has multiple applications and computation provides value in many ways:
·Design and optimization of a droplet generation microfluidic chips to control the size and release frequency of droplets for drug encapsulation.

· Design of inertial high throughput focusing chips for cell sorting (e.g. separation of blood cells and tumor cells)

· Design of DNA nano-translocation devices.

The benefits of MOEBIUS for microfluidics are

1

High accuracy modeling of single- and multi-species and phase fluids (DNS)

2

Handling of multi-physics and fluid-structure interaction

3

Automatic volume meshing

4

Fast turnaround times on multiple CPU or GPU – minutes/hours turnaround times vs. days/weeks
